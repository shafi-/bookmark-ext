Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2024

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how the Link Bookmarker Chrome Extension ("we", "our", or "the extension") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our extension.

Information Collection

Our extension collects and stores the following information:

  • Bookmarked URLs: The web addresses you choose to bookmark using our extension
  • Domain information: The domain names associated with your bookmarked URLs
  • User preferences: Your settings for the extension (such as icon visibility preferences)

Data Storage

All data collected by the extension is stored locally on your device using Chrome's local storage API. We do not transmit or store any of your data on external servers.

Data Usage

The information we collect is used solely for:

  • Providing bookmark functionality
  • Maintaining your bookmark list
  • Implementing user preferences

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or trade any of your information with third parties. All bookmarked data remains private and stored locally on your device.

Security

Your bookmarks and preferences are stored securely using Chrome's built-in storage mechanisms. The extension operates entirely locally, minimizing security risks associated with data transmission.

User Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your stored bookmarks at any time
  • Delete individual bookmarks or clear all stored data
  • Modify your extension preferences
  • Uninstall the extension, which will remove all stored data

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.

Contact

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please contact us through the Chrome Web Store support channel or create an issue on our GitHub repository.

Consent

By using the Link Bookmarker Chrome Extension, you consent to this Privacy Policy and our data handling practices as described above.
